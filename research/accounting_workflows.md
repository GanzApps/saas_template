# Accounting & Outsourcing Workflows: Big 4 Indonesia Focus
> Research build date: 2026-06-25
> Scope: Traditional and AI-implemented workflows for Accounting & Outsourcing at PwC Indonesia, EY Indonesia, and Deloitte Indonesia.
> Confidence/Evidence Score methodology: Each section rated **High**, **Medium**, or **Low** based on whether sources explicitly state Indonesia operations and specific workflow/tool names, or whether scope is inferred from global/Indonesia-listed service descriptions.

## 1. Traditional Workflow by Firm
*(Indonesia-listed service pages and FAO service descriptions)*

### 1.1 PwC Indonesia — Traditional FAO Workflow
PwC Indonesia offers Finance and Accounting Managed Services covering:
- **I2P (Invoice to Pay)** — vendor invoice processing and payment
- **O2C (Order to Cash)** — customer billing and receivables
- **R2R (Record to Report)** — journal posting, close, consolidation, statutory reporting aligned to IFAS/IFRS
- **Tax Compliance** — monthly/annual filings for Indonesian tax office (ITO)
- **Fixed Asset & Inventory accounting**
- **Payroll Processing**

Typical manual/enhanced mode described:
1. Document intake (invoice, PO, bank statement, tax invoice for e-Faktur)
2. Manual data capture and entry into accounting system
3. Matching PO vs. invoice vs. GRN (3-way match)
4. Exception handling and approval routing
5. Journal entry preparation and posting against General Ledger
6. Reconciliation (bank, intercompany, tax)
7. Month-end close, accruals, deferrals
8. Financial statement package preparation for IFRS/IFAS/US GAAP reporting
9. Statutory and regulatory filings
10. Dashboard/management reporting with stakeholder sign-off

**PwC Indonesia acceleration layer (tech-enabled):**
- PwC Indonesia describes a *24x7 Financial Reporting Compliance Test Factory* that incorporates "technology and automation every step of the way" — not fully AI-augmented, but includes RPA-driven testing and data acquisition.
- Workiva partnership and Financial Statements Assistant tooling (digitally upskilled resources in acceleration centers across five countries).

**Sources**
- PwC Indonesia Finance and Accounting Managed Services: https://www.pwc.com/id/en/services/accounting-advisory-services/finance-accounting-managed-services
- PwC Indonesia Financial Reporting Compliance: https://www.pwc.com/id/en/services/accounting-advisory-services/workiva/financial-reporting-compliance
- PwC Indonesia RPA CFO Survey 2020: https://www.pwc.com/id/en/pwc-publications/services-publications/assurance-publications/indonesia-cfo-survey-2020.html

**Confidence / Evidence Score: Medium**
- Indonesia-listed pages confirm traditional FAO scope and Test Factory concept, but do not present micro-level workflow steps (SOP numbers) for each sub-process.

### 1.2 EY Indonesia — Traditional FAO Workflow
EY Indonesia describes end-to-end assurance and advisory in Accounting Advisory Services across:
- **Financial Reporting Compliance** (statutory, regulatory)
- **Tax Compliance and Advisory**
- **Separation and Carve-out Accounting**
- **Process Improvement for accounting operations**

Traditional assurance workflow components documented:
1. Plan the audit using risk assessment and prior-year data
2. Obtain and store source documentation (contracts, ledgers, confirmations)
3. Execute fieldwork: tick-and-tie, substantive testing
4. Collect evidence and document conclusions
5. Review managerial sign-offs
6. Issue audit opinion and statutory filings
7. Post-close reporting and analytics package

EY Indonesia's audit approach keeps traditional competencies at its core (accounting, professional skepticism, teaming) augmented with analytics automation rather than purely automated from day one.

**Sources**
- EY Indonesia Audit Services: https://www.ey.com/en_id/services/audit
- EY Indonesia Audit Technology page: https://www.ey.com/en_id/services/audit/technology
- EY Indonesia AI Consulting Services (methodology emphasis): https://www.ey.com/en_id/services/consulting/artificial-intelligence-consulting-services

**Confidence / Evidence Score: Medium**
- Indonesia service page lists competency model; micro-steps inferred from global audit methodology, not uniquely Indonesia SOPs.

### 1.3 Deloitte Indonesia — Traditional FAO Workflow
Deloitte Indonesia (separate legal entities: Liana Ramon Xenia & Rekan, Deloitte Touche Solutions, PT Deloitte Konsultan Indonesia, PT Deloitte Advis Indonesia) operates across:
- **Record to Report (R2R)** — consolidations, statutory filings
- **Order to Cash (O2C)** — receivables and revenue accounting
- **Invoice to Pay (I2P)** — vendor payments and accruals
- **Tax Compliance** — direct and indirect tax assistance
- **FP&A** — planning, budgeting, forecasting support
- **Internal Audit / SOX** — risk-based testing

Traditional process flow (drawing from ISG FAO 2025 and Deloitte service descriptions):
1. Transaction processing & sub-ledger posting across multiple entities
2. Intercompany reconciliation and eliminations
3. Accruals and deferrals journals
4. Fixed asset register maintenance and depreciation runs
5. Bank and balance sheet reconciliations
6. Tax provision and compliance calculations
7. Statutory consolidation under Indonesian Financial Accounting Standards (IFAS-aligned to IFRS)
8. Management/board reporting pack assembly
9. Audit support and regulatory submission

Deloitte's traditional R2R emphasis (as characterized in ISG Provider Lens 2025): globally aligned processes, deep compliance and regulatory expertise, but not AI-first yet - service delivery includes manual close-cycle-heavy reviews pre-automation deployment.

**Sources**
- Deloitte ISG Provider Lens 2025 FAO eco-summary summarizing Deloitte: https://www.deloitte.com/global/en/about/recognition/analyst-relations/isg-provider-lens-finance-and-accounting-outsourcing-services.html

## 2. AI-Implemented Workflow by Firm

### 2.1 PwC Indonesia — AI-Enabled Workflow
PwC's global AI stack is referenced in Indonesia pages. Indonesia-explicit AI posture as of 2024:
- **"AI Beri Peluang Efisiensi dan Tantangan Keamanan Siber"** — PwC Indonesia has publicly discussed AI-driven efficiency together with cybersecurity risks, signaling AI adoption at the advisory level.
- Tool-based automation:
  - **AcceL8** (PwC's digital finance dashboard concept in region) — used for KPI visibility and acceleration.
  - **Financial Statements Assistant** — AI-assisted global reporting tool to ensure compliance and accelerate reporting.
  - **RPA + digital workforce** deployed broadly at PwC; Indonesia CFO Survey 2020 confirmed Indonesia CFOs leveraging RPA, showing local appetite and deployment pattern.
  - **Workiva + Test Factory** — automation-heavy compliance testing with acceleration centers.

AI workflow pattern (integrated):
1. Source documents digitized (OCR)
2. RPA extracts invoice fields into Workflow Automation engines (e.g., AcceL8 / internal platforms)
3. Auto reconciliation checks GL accounts against bank data
4. Exceptions flagged for human review with rules-based bot
5. Journal entries auto-prepare with human sign-off
6. Financial Statements Assistant produces multi-GAAP statements
7. Compliance review through Test Factory automation rail
8. Dashboard delivery to CFO with drill-down capability

**Sources**

- **AI Automation** — Deloitte's AI Automation unit supports complex task automation, end-to-end optimization, operations effectiveness
- **Generative AI in Finance Operate** — enhancing finance and accounting managed services for clients
- **Tax RPA** — routine repetitive tax compliance tasks automated

- **Agentic Process Automation (APA)** — combining RPA for structured tasks and AI agents for dynamic reasoning

1. OCR capture of invoice/payment documents
2. Bot-driven invoice validation, PO matching, variance flagging

4. GenAI drafts explanatory text and journal narratives
5. AI-driven reconciliation automation and exception routing
6. Autonomous close cycle with SuperLedger consolidating entity ledgers
7. GenAI assistant drafts financial commentary and board reports
8. Controls monitoring (SOX/IFAS compliance) via analytics automation

## 5. Integration Patterns

### 5.1 Cross-Platform Integration Model
All three firms integrate AI and automation into ERP-centric ecosystems:

1. **Data Layer** — OCR + RPA extract source document data into structured registers.
2. **Workflow Orchestration Layer** — EY Canvas, PwC AcceL8/dashboards, Deloitte orchestrators synchronize allocation, approval, and exception handling.
3. **ERP Transaction Layer** — Oracle (Deloitte SuperLedger), SAP (Deloitte + PwC), Workday, local ERP/QuickBooks-tier for smaller Indonesian clients.
4. **Analytics Layer** — EY Helix, PwC Workiva Test Factory, Power BI ecosystems link to underlying ERP datasets via APIs (EY Ponte service podcasts reference Power BI ↔ EY Canvas integration pattern).
5. **Reporting Layer** — Financial statement drafting (Financial Statements Assistant), audit opinion, and dashboarding back to CFO/audit committees.

### 5.2 Integration Vendors Referenced by Firm
- Microsoft (Azure, Foundry, Fabric, Power BI) — EY Indonesia
- Oracle Cloud Infrastructure — Deloitte SuperLedger
- SAP S/4HANA + AI/UX services — Deloitte + PwC ecosystem
- UiPath — Deloitte + PwC (RPA vendor)
- UiPath + PwC enterprise automation rollout to 50,000+ employees (global PwC reference)
